A NATURAL PHENOMENON

THE COLD LIGHT OF NATURE.

Fireflies are not flies. They are beetles that have mastered the art of organic chemistry to communicate through the dark.

DISCOVER THE SECRET
01. BIOLUMINESCENCE

ORGANIC CHEMISTRY IN FLIGHT.

Unlike a lightbulb, which wastes 90% of its energy as heat, a firefly’s light is 100% efficient. This "cold light" is produced when oxygen combines with calcium, adenosine triphosphate (ATP), and a chemical called luciferin in the presence of luciferase, a bioluminescent enzyme.

  • 100% Efficiency
  • 0% Heat Waste
Luciferin + O2 → Oxyluciferin + Light
02. COMMUNICATION

A LANGUAGE OF FLASHES.

Each species has a unique flash pattern. Some blink, some glow steadily, and some even synchronize their signals across entire forests. It's a high-stakes conversation used primarily for finding mates in the dense canopy of the night.

PRESERVING THE GLOW.

Light pollution and habitat loss are causing firefly populations to vanish. By reducing outdoor lighting, we can keep the night dark enough for their stories to continue.
